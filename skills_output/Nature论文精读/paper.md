## Abstract / 摘要

<a id="ABS001"></a>

**Original:**
Large language models offer novel opportunities to seek digital medical advice. While previous research primarily addressed the performance of such artificial intelligence (AI)-based tools, public perception of these advancements received little attention. In two preregistered studies (n = 2,280), we presented participants with scenarios of patients obtaining medical advice. All participants received identical information, but we manipulated the putative source of this advice ('AI', 'human physician', 'human + AI'). 'AI'- and 'human + AI'-labeled advice was evaluated as significantly less reliable and less empathetic compared with 'human'-labeled advice. Moreover, participants indicated lower willingness to follow the advice when AI was believed to be involved in advice generation. Our findings point toward an anti-AI bias when receiving digital medical advice, even when AI is supposedly supervised by physicians. Given the tremendous potential of AI for medicine, elucidating ways to counteract this bias should be an important objective of future research.

**中文:**
大型语言模型为寻求数字医疗建议提供了新的机遇。虽然以往研究主要关注此类基于人工智能（AI）的工具的性能表现，但公众对这些进展的感知却很少受到关注。在两项预注册研究中（n = 2,280），我们向参与者呈现了患者获取医疗建议的场景。所有参与者收到相同的信息，但我们操纵了该建议的声称来源（"AI"、"人类医生"、"人类 + AI"）。与"人类"标签的建议相比，标记为"AI"和"人类 + AI"的建议被评价为显著更不可靠且更缺乏同理心。此外，当参与者相信AI参与了建议生成时，他们表示遵循该建议的意愿较低。我们的发现表明，在接收数字医疗建议时存在一种反AI偏见，即使AI据称由医生监督也是如此。鉴于AI对医学的巨大潜力，阐明如何抵消这种偏见应当成为未来研究的重要目标。

**Note:** 这是一篇简短的通讯报告（Brief Communication），包含两项预注册实验。重点发现是：即使医疗建议内容完全相同，仅标签上的"AI"暗示就会导致参与者对建议的可靠性、同理心评价显著降低，且遵循意愿下降。

## Main / 正文

<a id="S001"></a>

**Original:**
Artificial intelligence (AI) holds enormous potential for the medical domain, for instance in analyzing medical images¹ or detecting drug interactions². Recent developments in the field of AI-based large language models (LLMs) have now given rise to numerous additional applications in healthcare. One use case that is becoming increasingly relevant from a public perspective is the use of LLMs when seeking medical advice³. To this end, popular LLM applications such as OpenAI's ChatGPT offer low-threshold access to medical information, seemingly without the need to consult specialist literature or professional physicians. While there are considerable concerns regarding the use of LLMs in healthcare⁴, earlier research indicates that ChatGPT 4.0 already achieves levels of diagnostic accuracy on a par with human physicians⁵. In another study, physicians (unaware of who created the advice) even rated LLM-generated responses to medical queries as superior in quality and more empathetic than answers generated by human physicians⁶.

**中文:**
人工智能（AI）在医学领域具有巨大潜力，例如在医学图像分析¹或检测药物相互作用²方面。基于AI的大型语言模型（LLM）的最新发展现在为医疗保健带来了众多额外应用。从公众角度来看，一个日益相关的用例是在寻求医疗建议时使用LLM³。为此，流行的LLM应用（如OpenAI的ChatGPT）提供了低门槛获取医疗信息的途径，似乎无需查阅专业文献或咨询专业医生。虽然对LLM在医疗保健中的使用存在相当大的担忧⁴，但早期研究表明，ChatGPT 4.0已经达到了与人类医生相当的诊断准确性水平⁵。在另一项研究中，医生（不知道建议由谁创建）甚至将LLM生成的医疗问题回答评为在质量和同理心方面优于人类医生生成的回答⁶。

**Note:** 段落建立背景——LLM在医疗领域的潜力与现状。引用6是关键锚点：Ayers et al. (2023) JAMA Intern. Med. 研究表明，当身份不公开时，LLM回答甚至被医生评为更优。

<a id="S002"></a>

**Original:**
AI-generated medical advice, thus, is perceived as high quality as long as the AI authorship is undisclosed. Yet, research from various domains indicates reservations against such content as soon as the AI authorship becomes apparent (algorithm aversion⁷). While similar effects have been observed for the use of AI-based tools in the medical field⁸, little is known about the perception of novel LLM applications. Moreover, previous research in this field often relied on relatively small samples, lacked an experimental study design (for example, refs. 9,10) or focused solely on the physician's perspective¹¹. However, from the public's point of view, not only the objective level of competence but also the subjective perception of the treating physician has a substantial influence on health-promoting behavior, treatment satisfaction and treatment outcome¹². Similarly, when seeking medical advice in digital settings, not only technical performance but also the public's perception of a new tool might prove decisive for its further dissemination and acceptance.

**中文:**
因此，只要AI作者身份未公开，AI生成的医疗建议就被认为是高质量的。然而，来自各个领域的研究表明，一旦AI作者身份变得明显，人们就会对此类内容产生保留态度（算法厌恶⁷）。虽然在医疗领域使用基于AI的工具方面也观察到了类似效应⁸，但对新型LLM应用的感知知之甚少。此外，该领域的先前研究通常依赖相对较小的样本、缺乏实验研究设计（例如参考文献9,10），或仅关注医生的视角¹¹。然而，从公众的角度来看，不仅是客观能力水平，对治疗医生的主观感知也对促进健康行为、治疗满意度和治疗结果有重大影响¹²。同样，在数字环境中寻求医疗建议时，不仅是技术性能，公众对新工具的感知也可能决定其进一步传播和接受。

**Note:** "Algorithm aversion"（算法厌恶）是Dietvorst et al. (2015)提出的经典概念，指人们一旦发现算法犯错后，会错误地避免使用算法。此处将其扩展到LLM医疗建议领域。

<a id="S003"></a>

**Original:**
Within our work, we thus aim to explore the public's perspective on LLM-generated medical advice in a controlled, experimental setting. Therefore, we conducted two preregistered experiments with large samples (study 1: n = 1,050 across various nationalities; study 2: n = 1,230, representative of the UK population in terms of age, gender and ethnicity). Within both studies, we investigated how labeling identical medical advice as generated either by a human physician or by an AI-supported chatbot affects how this information is perceived in terms of reliability, comprehensibility and empathy. As it is expected that AI will not replace but rather support human competencies in the future¹³, we further extend previous research by including a third group in which the information was labeled as generated by a human physician in collaboration with AI. In study 2, we additionally measured the individual willingness to follow the provided advice. Moreover, we assessed participants' interest in testing the tool, which supposedly generated the previously encountered medical information by offering the opportunity to save a (fictious) link to a corresponding platform. Thus, our research abstracts from potential differences in the quality of AI- versus human-generated medical information. Instead, we focus on illuminating potential biases toward novel LLM-based tools as sources of medical advice from the public's perspective.

**中文:**
因此，在我们的工作中，我们旨在通过受控实验环境探索公众对LLM生成医疗建议的看法。为此，我们进行了两项预注册实验，样本量较大（研究1：n = 1,050，涵盖多种国籍；研究2：n = 1,230，在年龄、性别和种族方面代表英国人口）。在两项研究中，我们考察了将相同的医疗建议标记为由人类医生或AI支持的聊天机器人生成，如何影响该信息在可靠性、可理解性和同理心方面的感知。由于预期AI未来不会取代而是支持人类能力¹³，我们进一步扩展了先前研究，增加了第三组——该组信息被标记为由人类医生与AI合作生成。在研究2中，我们额外测量了个人遵循所提供建议的意愿。此外，我们通过提供保存（虚构的）对应平台链接的机会，评估了参与者测试声称生成先前医疗信息的工具的兴趣。因此，我们的研究抽离了AI生成与人类生成医疗信息质量的潜在差异，而是聚焦于从公众视角阐明对基于LLM的新型工具作为医疗建议来源的潜在偏见。

**Note:** 关键的实验设计信息：三项实验条件（人类、AI、人类+AI），研究2增加了"遵循建议意愿"和"保存链接"行为指标。所有参与者看到的是完全相同的医疗建议内容，仅标签不同。

There was no difference of empathy ratings between the 'AI' and 'human + AI' condition, t < 1. Reliability ratings differed significantly between different author labels, F(2, 1,047) = 9.68, P < 0.001, ηp² = 0.02. 'Human' advice was rated as significantly more reliable than 'AI' advice, t(698) = 3.72, P < 0.001, d = 0.28 95% CI (0.13, 0.43), and 'human + AI' advice, t(698) = 3.90, P < 0.001, d = 0.29 95% CI (0.15, 0.44). <a id="S005"></a>

**Original:**
Our findings may be based on a stronger association of the 'human physician' label with a mutual demonstration of care and respect, which are vital factors for successful patient–physician interactions¹⁴. Hence, our results reinforce that the public perceives physicians as more appropriate sources of medical information than AI-based tools. This outcome is in line with earlier findings on algorithm aversion⁷, particularly within the medical domain (for example, ref. 11). Conversely, the use of AI may have been perceived as 'dehumanizing'¹⁵, a sentiment highlighted by the lower empathy scores for AI-labeled advice in both of our studies. A further explanation for the observed resistance to AI-generated medical advice might be the phenomenon of 'uniqueness neglect', wherein users believe AI may not adequately consider their individual characteristics. Consequently, explaining that even AI-generated advice processes and considers personal information provided by the individuals themselves could potentially increase acceptance¹⁶.

**中文:**
我们的发现可能基于"人类医生"标签与相互展示关怀和尊重之间更强的关联，而关怀和尊重是成功的患者-医生互动的关键因素¹⁴。因此，我们的结果强化了公众认为医生比基于AI的工具是更合适的医疗信息来源的观点。这一结果与早期关于算法厌恶的发现一致⁷，特别是在医疗领域（例如参考文献11）。相反，AI的使用可能被视为"去人性化"¹⁵，这一情绪由两项研究中AI标签建议的同理心评分较低所凸显。对观察到的AI生成医疗建议抵触的另一种解释可能是"独特性忽视"现象，即用户认为AI可能无法充分考虑他们的个体特征。因此，解释即使是AI生成的建议也会处理并考虑个人自身提供的信息，可能有助于增加接受度¹⁶。

**Note:** 讨论部分开始。提出三种解释机制：(1) 人类医生与关怀/尊重的强关联；(2) AI被视为"去人性化"；(3) "独特性忽视"（uniqueness neglect）——Longoni et al. (2019) 提出的概念。

<a id="S006"></a>

**Original:**
Our observation that human-labeled medical advice was not perceived as significantly more comprehensible than AI-labeled information indicates that the label effect exerted less influence on this dimension compared with factors such as reliability and empathy. Possibly, this dimension was perceived as more technical in nature and therefore less critical for sensitive medical settings. In this respect, AI could therefore perform on an equivalent level with human physicians. This observation is noteworthy as it indicates that AI was not generally evaluated more 'negatively', which can occur when judgment of one feature influences judgments of other features of the same evaluation object, known as halo effect¹⁷.

**中文:**
我们观察到，人类标签的医疗建议在可理解性方面并未被认为显著优于AI标签的信息，这表明标签效应对该维度的影响小于对可靠性和同理心等因素的影响。可能该维度在本质上被认为更具技术性，因此在敏感的医疗环境中不那么关键。在这方面，AI可以与人类医生表现相当。这一观察值得注意，因为它表明AI并未被普遍评价得更为"负面"——这种负面评价可能发生在对一个特征的判断影响对同一评价对象其他特征的判断时，即所谓的晕轮效应¹⁷。

**Note:** 可理解性维度无标签效应的重要讨论——AI未被"全面"负面评价，说明偏见是领域特定的（针对可靠性和同理心），而非泛化的负面态度。这反驳了晕轮效应的存在。

<a id="S008"></a>

**Original:**
Both of our studies have limitations. First, to ensure high internal validity, participants in the experiments had to adopt the perspective of other individuals and therefore were unable to formulate their own inquiries. Moreover, the examined dialogs consisted of only one incoming question and a single subsequent response. Thus, our chosen setting was representative of brief interactions occurring on a digital medical platform, while not capturing extensive interactions as those in face-to-face doctor–patient consultations. We consider such a more interactive but also less controlled environment as an intriguing approach for future research.

**中文:**
我们的两项研究都有局限性。首先，为了确保较高的内部效度，实验中的参与者必须采用他人的视角，因此无法提出自己的询问。此外，所检查的对话仅包含一个问题和一个后续回复。因此，我们选择的设置代表了发生在数字医疗平台上的简短互动，但未捕捉到如面对面医患咨询中那样的广泛互动。我们认为这种更具互动性但也更不受控的环境是未来研究的一个有趣方向。

Our findings indicate a bias against medical advice labeled as AI-generated, regardless of additional supervision by human physicians. Along the same lines, previous research has shown that the public's reservations toward medical AI persist even when AI-generated content is medically supervised¹⁸. Considering the expected surge of AI in healthcare and the immense potential for human–AI collaboration, this finding raises notable concerns. To address this bias, in addition to the general public, other stakeholders, such as physicians and insurance providers, will need to be engaged accordingly. Interestingly, another study showed that if people were assured that humans would remain unequivocally in the decision-making position, the combination of human and AI achieved significantly higher levels of trust than without this assurance¹⁹. Consequently, the specific framing of the involvement of AI in generating and delivering medical advice may be pivotal for its public acceptance.

**中文:**
我们的发现表明，无论是否有额外的人类医生监督，对标记为AI生成的医疗建议都存在偏见。同样，先前研究表明，即使AI生成的内容经过医学监督，公众对医疗AI的保留态度仍然存在¹⁸。考虑到AI在医疗保健中的预期激增以及人机协作的巨大潜力，这一发现引发了值得关注的担忧。为应对这种偏见，除了公众之外，还需要相应地吸引医生和保险提供者等其他利益相关者参与。有趣的是，另一项研究表明，如果人们确信人类将明确保留在决策位置，人类和AI的组合会比没有这种保证时获得显著更高的信任水平¹⁹。因此，AI参与生成和传递医疗建议的具体框架可能对其公众接受度至关重要。

**Note:** 结论段落。核心信息：(1) 反AI偏见即使有医生监督也存在；(2) 需要多方利益相关者参与；(3) 关键解决方案——"人类在决策环中"的保证（Aoki, 2021）可显著提升信任，提示"框架效应"在未来AI医疗中的重要性。

##### Case reports / 病例报告

**Original:**
The case reports used in this study address four distinct medical topics: smoking cessation, colonoscopy, agoraphobia and reflux disease (Supplementary Figs. 1–4). ##### Scales / 量表

**Original:**
Participants evaluated the presented case reports regarding perceived reliability, comprehensibility and empathy. By using these categories, we closely adhered to existing literature on key evaluation criteria from the patient's perspective in doctor–patient interactions (see refs. 6,21 for 'reliability' and 'empathy' and ref. 22 for 'comprehensibility'). Moreover, these three dimensions allowed us to cover different facets of medical dialogs in a reasonably comprehensive and distinct manner. With 'reliability', we addressed the assessment of the content of the medical advice (content-related component). With 'comprehensibility', we recorded the public understandability and how accessible the information was structured (format-related component). Finally, with 'empathy', we captured the transfer of information on an emotional interpersonal level (interaction-related component). As no established survey instruments with practice-proven suitability for the present research question exist, we developed novel scales closely aligned with best practices in this field. That is, we decided on a relatively low number of response options with individual, unambiguous labels and used symmetrical scales with nonoverlapping categories²³˙²⁴. The final 7-point Likert scales went from 'extremely unreliable' to 'extremely reliable', from 'extremely difficult to understand' to 'extremely easy to understand' and from 'extremely unempathic' to 'extremely empathic'.

**中文:**
参与者对所呈现的病例报告在感知可靠性、可理解性和同理心方面进行了评估。通过使用这些类别，我们紧密遵循了现有文献中关于医患互动中患者视角的关键评估标准（关于"可靠性"和"同理心"见参考文献6,21，关于"可理解性"见参考文献22）。此外，这三个维度使我们能够以相当全面和清晰的方式涵盖医疗对话的不同方面。通过"可靠性"，我们处理了对医疗建议内容的评估（内容相关成分）。通过"可理解性"，我们记录了公众的可理解性以及信息结构化的可及性（格式相关成分）。最后，通过"同理心"，我们捕捉了情感人际层面的信息传递（互动相关成分）。由于不存在针对当前研究问题具有实践验证适用性的既定调查工具，我们开发了与该领域最佳实践紧密对齐的新量表。即，我们选择了相对较少的回答选项，每个选项有独立、明确的标签，并使用具有非重叠类别的对称量表²³˙²⁴。最终的7点Likert量表从"极其不可靠"到"极其可靠"，从"极其难以理解"到"极其容易理解"，以及从"极其缺乏同理心"到"极其富有同理心"。

**Note:** 三个维度各自对应医患沟通的不同层面：可靠性（内容）、可理解性（格式）、同理心（人际互动）。使用7点Likert量表。量表的效度通过AI态度相关性得到支持。

#### Experimental design and procedure / 实验设计与程序

**Original:**
We used a unifactorial between-subject design, with the manipulated factor being the supposed author of the presented medical information (human, AI, human + AI; Supplementary Fig. 5). Participants were instructed to carefully read all scenarios that were presented in random order. Afterward, we assessed participants' attitudes toward AI. Hence, we inquired about their frequency of using AI-based tools (response options: never, rarely, occasionally, frequently, very frequently), their perception of the impact of AI on healthcare (response options: no, minor, moderate, significant, highly significant) and whether they view the integration of AI in healthcare as presenting more risks or opportunities (response options: more risks, neutral, more opportunities). Finally, we collected demographic information on gender, age, educational level and nationality.

**中文:**
我们采用单因素被试间设计，操纵因素是所呈现医疗信息的声称作者（人类、AI、人类 + AI；补充图5）。参与者被指示仔细阅读所有以随机顺序呈现的场景。之后，我们评估了参与者对AI的态度。因此，我们询问了他们使用基于AI的工具的频率（回答选项：从不、很少、偶尔、频繁、非常频繁），他们对AI对医疗保健影响的感知（回答选项：无、轻微、中等、显著、非常显著），以及他们认为AI在医疗保健中的整合是带来更多风险还是更多机遇（回答选项：更多风险、中立、更多机遇）。最后，我们收集了有关性别、年龄、教育水平和国籍的人口统计信息。

#### Data treatment and analyses / 数据处理与分析

**Original:**
We preregistered our analysis plan, data collection strategy and the experimental design (https://osf.io/6trux). Data analysis was conducted in R version 4.1.1 (R Core Team). A separate analysis of variance was calculated for each rating dimension (reliability, comprehensibility, empathy), using the supposed author of the medical advice as a between-subject factor (human, AI, human + AI). Significant main effects were followed by two-sample t-tests (two-tailed), comparing all factor levels. Cohen's d is reported as a measure of effect size, which is calculated with the t_out function of the schoRsch package version 1.10 in R (ref. 25). <a id="M009"></a>

#### Materials and procedure / 材料与程序

**Original:**
Within our second experiment, we used the same case reports as for study 1. Again, we used a unifactorial between-subject design, with the manipulated factor being the supposed author of the presented medical information (human, AI, human + AI; Supplementary Fig. 5). However, in contrast to study 1, the author label was manipulated only via text instead of via additional symbols. The experimental procedure was similar to that of study 1, but we used two additional measures of preference. Thus, in addition to perceived reliability, comprehensibility and empathy, we also measured the individual willingness to follow the provided advice. To further test the robustness of our survey instruments, we also slightly adapted the scales on which participants rated the respective dimensions. That is, we used 5-point Likert scales (instead of the 7-point scales used in study 1), going from 'very unreliable' to 'very reliable', from 'very difficult to understand' to 'very easy to understand', from 'very unempathic' to 'very empathic' and from 'very unwilling' to 'very willing'. Moreover, at the end of the experiment, participants had the opportunity to save a (fictious) link to the platform and tool, which supposedly generated the previously encountered responses. This tool was framed depending on the experimental condition ('The previous scenarios where exemplary conversations from a digital platform where users can engage in conversations with a licensed medical doctor (an AI-supported chatbot) regarding medical queries. (All responses on this platform are reviewed by a licensed medical doctor and may be supplemented or revised if necessary.)'). Participants could save this link by clicking on a corresponding button. For each rating dimension, there was a positive relation with the decision to save the link, Ps ≤ 0.012. Moreover, similar to study 1, for the AI condition, attitudes toward AI (perceived opportunities and impact) were positively correlated with ratings in each domain, Ps ≤ 0.001, thus again supporting the validity of our scales. At the end of the study, we again queried participants' attitudes toward AI and demographic information. In addition, we also assessed participants' patient status ('Based on your current health status, would you describe yourself as a patient?'; response options: yes, no, prefer not to say) and whether they work in a healthcare-related profession or received a healthcare-related training ('Based on your training or current profession, would you describe yourself as a healthcare professional?'; response options: yes, no, prefer not to say). If the latter question was answered with 'yes', participants could also indicate their exact profession. Finally, as an attention check, we asked participants who the stated source of the provided medical responses was ('a licensed medical doctor', 'an AI-supported chatbot', 'an AI-supported chatbot, revised and supplemented by a licensed medical doctor').

**中文:**

#### Data treatment and analyses / 数据处理与分析

**Original:**
We preregistered our analysis plan, data collection strategy and the experimental design (https://osf.io/wn6mj). Again, data analysis was conducted in R version 4.1.1 (R Core Team). For each rating dimension (reliability, comprehensibility, empathy, willingness to follow), a similar mixed-effect regression analysis was calculated as for study 1. Significant treatment effects were followed by two-sample t-tests (two-tailed), comparing all factor levels. Similar to study 1, Cohen's d is reported as a measure of effect size. Furthermore, we calculated a binomial logistic regression of the decision to press the 'save link' button (yes or no), using the author label condition (human, AI, human + AI) as a fixed factor and the individual participant as a random factor (intercept). The author label condition was dummy coded with the 'human' condition as the reference category. We report absolute values for all statistics and P values were calculated using Satterthwaite's method. Again, the Holm–Bonferroni method was applied to account for multiple testing.

**中文:**

## 阅读提示 / Critical Reading Notes

1. **效应量解释：** 虽然所有主要发现均达到统计显著性，但效应量较小（Cohen's d ≈ 0.21–0.31，ηp² = 0.02）。这意味着标签效应仅解释了约2%的方差，实际意义需结合临床情境谨慎解读。

2. **实验与现实场景的差距：** 参与者以第三人称视角阅读场景，并非真实求医。在实际健康焦虑情境下，反AI偏见可能被放大或减弱。

3. **"人类+AI"标签未缓解偏见：** 这是研究最引人注目的发现之一。即使告知参与者AI回复经过了医生审查和补充，偏见依然存在。这与Aoki (2021)的发现形成对比——后者表明"人类保留决策权"的保证可提高信任。

4. **AI与人类+AI组无差异：** 提示公众可能将任何涉及AI的标签等同于"AI生成"，而忽略了"人类监督"的附加信息。

5. **行为指标与态度指标的分离：** 保存链接的行为无标签效应，但评分有标签效应——这提示外显态度和内隐行为可能存在分离。

6. **补充材料缺失：** 补充图1-5和补充结果无法从PMC全文XML中提取，需直接查阅Nature网站或OSF仓库获取。
